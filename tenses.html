Passé Composé
The Passé Composé is used to express actions that have been completed in the past. It is formed using the auxiliary verbs 'avoir' or 'être' followed by the past participle of the main verb.

auxiliary verb conjugated + past participle of infinitive verb

Most verbs use 'avoir' as the auxiliary verb.

  • Verb of movement (aller, venir, etc.), use 'être' as the auxiliary verb.
  • Reflexive Verbs (se lever, se regarder, etc) use 'être' as the auxiliary verb.
  • The past participle must agree with the subject when 'être' is used as the auxiliary verb.
  • Example: J'ai mangé. (I ate.) / Elle est allée. (She went.)

Futur Proche
The Futur Proche is used to express actions that are going to happen soon.

aller (present tense) + infinitive verb

  • The verb 'aller' must be conjugated in the present tense.
  • The main verb remains in its infinitive form.
  • Example: Je vais partir. (I am going to leave.) / Nous allons manger. (We are going to eat.)

Futur Simple
The Futur Simple is used to describe actions that will happen in the future.

ainfinitive verb + (ai,as,a,ons,ez,ont)

Conjugation of Futur Simple

PronounConjugation

Nousons
Vousez
Ils/Ellesont
  • Verbs with a grave accent on the stem's short "e":
    • peser – je pèserai
    • modeler – je modèlerai
  • Many verbs ending in -eler or -eter double the final consonants:
    • jeter – je jetterai
  • Verbs that lose the i in the ending -rir before adding the future ending:
    • courir – je courrai
    • mourir – je mourrai
  • Verbs ending in -yer change the y to an i in the future tense (with an exception for -ayer where both are allowed):
    • employer – j’emploierai, tu emploieras, il emploiera, nous emploierons, vous emploierez, ils emploieront
    • payer – je payerai/paierai
  • Irregular verbs ending in -oir, as well as aller, envoyer, faire, and venir, follow irregular patterns:
    • pouvoir – je pourrai, tu pourras, il pourra, nous pourrons, vous pourrez, ils pourront
      • Example: Je parlerai. (I will speak.) / Nous finirons. (We will finish.)
Passé Récent
The Passé Récent is used to express actions that have just happened. It is formed using the present tense of 'venir' followed by 'de' and the infinitive of the main verb.

venir (present tense) + de + infinitive verb

  • Example: Je viens de manger. (I just ate.) / Il vient de partir. (He just left.)
